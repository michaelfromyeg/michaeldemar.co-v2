Graphic Design

CAPABLE GRAPHIC DESIGNER THAT LEARNED BY DOING.
  • First became interested in graphic design after taking a mix of visual communications and coding classes in junior high, working with Photoshop and Illustrator to create simple projects.
  • In high school, I found infinite room to play, design, and flourish as a designer. I created:
    • advertisements for our school’s Pancake Breakfast,
    • branding for our Bike-a-Thon, Junior High Leadership Conference (in which I worked with Karen Schiltz, graphic designer for Classic Landscapes), Heritage Festival,
    • and for my initiatives, such as our Book Club, our volunteering club, MASSIVE, and my personal website. I even got approached by our school’s media team to create a logo, which is now used by our photograph teacher as an email signature.
  • Assisted with branding for Canadian Student Leadership Conference (CSLC) 2018. I worked with a talented graphic designer contracted by Harry Ainlay who walked me through the design process. He worked with my logo designs when eventually drafting our final conference branding.
  • For Student Senate, I was a member of the ‘Time Management’ committee, whose primary focus was creating a resource for students to combat procrastination, for which we decided brochures were best suited. I took on the responsbility of designing the brochures. I used Illustrator, Photoshop, and InDesign to create them. Operating under certain constraints, such as a maximum of three colors, and an amount of content to fit, the brochures were complete. We printed off 600 copies for each school in EPSB.
